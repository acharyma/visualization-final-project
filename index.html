About the Visualization

The Bar Graph plots the Income Per Capita for each of the different district in Nepal. The bar graph has a sort function that toggles between ascending and descending order. When a Bar is clicked, that district gets highlighted on the bar graph, and the line chart displays the progression of dropout rates in that district throughout the different grades. We set it to default to Kathmandu, Nepal because it is the capital city/district of the country. The selected district are also saved for when the map is revealed. When the map is revealed, there is a blue outline on the selected districts (from the Bar Graph). As of now, the map only shows the region and district on hover, but once we merge the three datasets we use to one (for the final product), we will display the information about that district in full (including dropouts, total number of teachers, life expectancy, etc) when a district is clicked. We also made the border of the district red to distinguish between the interested districts and the districts actually looked at. If time permits, we want to try and get a map of the United States to compare the dropout rates in the different states and compare it with the different districts for the final project.
