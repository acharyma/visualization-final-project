Our Process

The most difficult aspect of our project was data processing. We used two seperate csv files for this project. One contained information on Life Expectancy and Income Per Capita, and the other had information on Dropout Rates by grade. Both were grouped by the district in Nepal so we knew we wanted to look at these information based on the district. On click on the bar, we added a callback that sent back to main.js the district name, and then we called the update function of the linechart with the district variable, and the OTHER dataset.

More than processing the CSV file, the most difficult and time consuming process was going through the nepal.json file and manually adding the district names under the properties for each district. The dataset came with it's own district names under the VARNAME_3 property, but only about 5 of the districts had that property filled out. Ironically, those 5 districts were all spelt differently compared to the opennepaldata site [which was our two datasets]. So we had to click on each of the districts on the map, find it's ID number, find the district name on a map, compare the spelling to our dataset, and add the correct district name accordingly. This process needed to be meticulous to ensure no mistakes and it took a very long time to process.

For our design aspect, we wanted to make sure that the line chart was between the bar chart and the map, because the interactive feature of both the bar graph and the map was changing that line chart in the middle. We also had to decide on the color scheme for the website and the graphs. We liked the purple and grey scheme for the titles and the bodies. As you move further into the website, we started moving from purple towards the red/orange scale.
